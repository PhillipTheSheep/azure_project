Here are all the charts used for the project.

Zoom out to adjust dashboard dimensions.

Description 1: This pie chart shows the ratio of products sold by category. Commodities make up over a quarter of all revenue made meaning this is where groceries stores make a huge portion of their money.

Description 2: This graph shows household spending against the purchased date. There is a peak in the month of April 2020 where most States started shutting down. We assisted on supplies shortage within that period, causing a downward trend in 2020

Description 3: This chart shows the sum of spending by age range. The 45-55 makes up the largest spending block meaning grocery stores may want to appeal to this age range specifically.

Description 4: This chart shows the sum of spending by income range. The 50-74k income range makes up the largest spending block meaning grocery stores may want to appeal to this income range specifically.

Description 5: This chart shows the sum of household spending by household size. Families of 2 seem to spend roughly the same amount per person as other families but as you can see, families of 2 make up the largest spending group. This is likely because there are more families of 2 than other sizes of families.

Description 6: This graph displays the total household spending in 2019. We manifested a decrease on year 2020 as the world witnessed a global pandemic. People losing their jobs and facing hardships could be major factors to that decrease.

Description 3: This chart shows specific customer engagement over time. We use this to see or track changes to a household that could increase or decrease spending over time.
