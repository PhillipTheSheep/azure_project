How does customer engagement change over time?

Figure 1. This graph displays the total household spending in 2019. We manifested a decrease on year 2020 as the world witnessed a global pandemic. People losing their jobs and facing hardships could be major factors to that decrease.


Figure 2. This graph shows household spending against the purchased date. There is a peak in the month of April 2020 where most States started shutting down. We assisted on supplies shortage within that period, causing a downward trend in 2020


Figure 3. This chart shows specific customer engagement over time. We use this to see or track changes to a household that could increase or decrease spending over time.


Which demographic factors (e.g. household size, presence of children, income) appear to affect customer engagement?

Figure 4. This chart shows the sum of household spending by household size. Families of 2 seem to spend roughly the same amount per person as other families but as you can see, families of 2 make up the largest spending group. This is likely because there are more families of 2 than other sizes of families.


Figure 5. This chart shows the sum of spending by age range. The 45-55 makes up the largest spending block meaning grocery stores may want to appeal to this age range specifically.


Figure 6. This chart shows the sum of spending by income range. The 50-74k income range makes up the largest spending block meaning grocery stores may want to appeal to this income range specifically.


Figure 7. This pie chart shows the ratio of products sold by category. Commodities make up over a quarter of all revenue made meaning this is where groceries stores make a huge portion of their money.
